Research Papers

  • Making the Elite: Discrimination at Top Firms

    • Short Summary: Twitter thread
    Coverage: Breaking the Mold: India's Untraveled Path to Prosperity (Raghuram Rajan); The Global Spectator; Marginal Revolution


    How quantitatively salient is hiring discrimination in driving socioeconomic gaps in access to elite jobs, and where do disparities emerge in the hiring process? Using recruitment data from over 1000 jobs at elite multinational corporations in the U.S. and Europe that hire from an elite Indian college, I find that nearly 90% of socioeconomic (caste) disparities occur in the final screening round, focused on personal interviews assessing "cultural fit." At this stage, gatekeepers at elite firms primarily apply socially constructed perceptions of merit to include or exclude candidates based on caste, perpetuating systemic inequality and socioeconomic disadvantage. This bias, which arises as soon as caste is revealed, is unjustified by performance, as disadvantaged castes have a nearly 20% higher promotion rate, even in the most selective jobs. These findings underscore challenges for Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI) in organizations and reveal how organizational behavior maintains unequal power over, and access to, elite roles in society.
